First, I extract the metadata from the photo (specifically, an iPhone shot)
 using ExifTool to obtain the GPS coordinates and essential investigation data.
 Next, I pivot to the browser and use Google Maps to verify the coordinates.
 Once the location is confirmed, I use Maltego to map out the connections based on the extracted data.
 By analyzing the sender's username, I successfully uncovered their Instagram account, which revealed additional context:
 the photo session was taking place with their school/university class.
 This entire workflow was built on metadata analysis and Google Dorks—proving to be a highly
 effective way to geolocate an image even when there are no visible landmarks to rely on.
 This concludes the GEOINT case study.
